ACTIVE & PASSIVE VOICE 🇬🇧 CHAPTER 3
Understanding the difference and how to transform between active and passive voice
🇬🇧

Active & Passive Voice

Active voice · Passive voice · Transformation rules · Verb conjugations

Complete lesson

📚 INTRODUCTION

Active Voice describes a sentence where the subject performs the action stated by the verb.

In Passive Voice sentences, the subject is acted upon by the verb.

📌 Basic Structure:
Active: Subject + Verb + Object
Passive: Object + be + past participle + (by + subject)

📊 ACTIVE vs PASSIVE VOICE

Active Voice

The subject performs the action

The dog bit the man.
She writes a letter.
They built a house.

Passive Voice

The subject receives the action

The man was bitten by the dog.
A letter is written by her.
A house was built by them.

🔄 TRANSFORMATION RULES

TenseActive VoicePassive Voice
Present SimpleShe writes a letterA letter is written by her
Present ContinuousShe is writing a letterA letter is being written by her
Present PerfectShe has written a letterA letter has been written by her
Past SimpleShe wrote a letterA letter was written by her
Past ContinuousShe was writing a letterA letter was being written by her
Past PerfectShe had written a letterA letter had been written by her
Future Simple (will)She will write a letterA letter will be written by her
Future Simple (going to)She is going to write a letterA letter is going to be written by her
Modal verbs (can, must, etc.)She can write a letterA letter can be written by her

💡 WHEN TO USE PASSIVE VOICE

📌 We use passive voice when:

- The agent (doer) is unknown or unimportant
My wallet was stolen. (I don't know who stole it)

- We want to emphasize the action or the receiver
The president was elected in 2020.

- In formal or scientific writing
The experiment was conducted under controlled conditions.

⚠️ WHEN TO AVOID PASSIVE VOICE

📌 Avoid passive voice when:

- The active voice is clearer and more direct
- You want to be more engaging (especially in narrative writing)
- The agent is important to the meaning

Better (Active): Shakespeare wrote Hamlet.
Less effective (Passive): Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.

❌ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

📌 Mistakes:

- Forgetting the "be" verb
❌ The letter written by her.
✅ The letter was written by her.

- Wrong past participle form
❌ The cake was ate.
✅ The cake was eaten.

💡 SUMMARY & KEY POINTS

📌 Key Takeaways:
- Active Voice: Subject performs the action (Subject + Verb + Object)
- Passive Voice: Subject receives the action (Object + be + past participle + by + subject)
- The passive voice is formed with "be" + past participle
- Use passive voice when the agent is unknown or unimportant
- Use passive voice to emphasize the action or receiver
- Avoid passive voice when active voice is clearer and more direct
- Only transitive verbs (verbs that take an object) can be used in passive voice
