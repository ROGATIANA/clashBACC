GRAMMAR: EITHER/OR VS NEITHER/NOR 🇬🇧 CHAPTER 5
Using either/or and neither/nor correctly · Subject-verb agreement rules
🇬🇧

Either/Or vs Neither/Nor

Affirmative vs negative · Singular vs plural agreement · Sentence structure

Complete lesson

📚 INTRODUCTION

Either/or and neither/nor are correlative conjunctions used to connect two or more options. They are essential for expressing choices and negations in English.

📌 Quick Reference:
- Either/or → positive choices (this OR that)
- Neither/nor → negative choices (not this AND not that)

✅ EITHER / OR

📌 Usage:
Used in a sentence in the affirmative sense when referring to a choice between two possibilities.

Structure: either + option A + or + option B

We can either eat now or after the show - it's up to you.
You can either pay by cash or by credit card.
Either we will go out, or we will stay at home.

❌ NEITHER / NOR

📌 Usage:
Used in a sentence in the negative sense when you want to say that two or more things are not true.

Structure: neither + option A + nor + option B

Neither my mother nor my father went to university.
He speaks neither English nor French.
Neither of the restaurants is open today.

📏 SUBJECT-VERB AGREEMENT RULES

Rule 1

Both elements singular → verb singular

Either the father or the mother has to attend the meeting.
Neither Leila nor Nancy is going to write the report.

Rule 2

One element plural → verb plural

Either Sue or the girls are going to prepare dinner tonight.
Neither the teacher nor the students were in the classroom.
📌 Proximity Rule (Rule of Proximity):
The verb agrees with the element closest to it (the one right before the verb).

Either the students or the teacher is responsible.
(teacher is singular, so verb is "is")

Either the teacher or the students are responsible.
(students is plural, so verb is "are")

📝 USING EITHER AND NEITHER AT THE BEGINNING OF A SENTENCE

📌 Either at the beginning:
Either we will go out, or we will stay at home.
Either you pay the bill, or I will.
📌 Neither at the beginning:
Neither of the restaurants is open today.
Neither of them is available.
Neither answer is correct.

📊 QUICK COMPARISON TABLE

Either/Or

✓ Affirmative
✓ Choice between two options
✓ "One or the other"

Neither/Nor

✗ Negative
✗ Both options are false
✗ "Not one and not the other"

⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

📌 Mistake 1: Using "nor" without "neither"
❌ I don't like coffee nor tea.
✅ I like neither coffee nor tea.
✅ I don't like coffee or tea.
📌 Mistake 2: Double negative with "neither...nor"
❌ I don't like neither coffee nor tea.
✅ I like neither coffee nor tea.
📌 Mistake 3: Verb agreement errors
❌ Neither the manager nor the employees is happy.
✅ Neither the manager nor the employees are happy.
(employees is plural, so verb is "are")

✏️ PRACTICE EXAMPLES

Example 1

You can ___ pay now ___ later.
→ either / or

Example 2

___ John ___ Mary likes broccoli.
→ Neither / nor

Example 3

___ the red shirt ___ the blue one looks good.
→ Neither / nor

Example 4

You can ___ call me ___ send an email.
→ either / or

💡 SUMMARY & KEY POINTS

📌 Key Takeaways:
- Either/or = choice between two possibilities (affirmative)
- Neither/nor = both options are false (negative)
- Both elements singular → singular verb
- One element plural → plural verb (proximity rule)
- "Either" and "neither" can start a sentence
- Don't use "nor" without "neither"
- Avoid double negatives with "neither...nor"
