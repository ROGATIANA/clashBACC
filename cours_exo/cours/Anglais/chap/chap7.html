English Tenses

Present · Past · Future · Perfect · Continuous · Affirmative · Negative · Questions

Complete lesson

📚 INTRODUCTION

English verb tenses indicate when an action occurs (past, present, future) and how it occurs (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous). Mastering tenses is essential for clear and accurate communication.

📌 Three main time frames:
- Present : actions happening now or regularly
- Past : actions already completed
- Future : actions that will happen

📊 TENSE OVERVIEW

Tense Affirmative Negative Question Use
Present Simple I work I don't work Do I work? habits, facts, routines
Present Continuous I am working I am not working Am I working? actions happening now
Present Perfect I have worked I haven't worked Have I worked? past → present connection
Present Perfect Continuous I have been working I haven't been working Have I been working? duration from past to now
Past Simple I worked I didn't work Did I work? completed past actions
Past Continuous I was working I wasn't working Was I working? actions in progress in the past
Past Perfect I had worked I hadn't worked Had I worked? past before another past
Past Perfect Continuous I had been working I hadn't been working Had I been working? duration before a past event
Future Simple (will) I will work I won't work Will I work? predictions, promises, decisions
Future (going to) I am going to work I am not going to work Am I going to work? plans, intentions
Future Continuous I will be working I won't be working Will I be working? actions in progress in the future
Future Perfect I will have worked I won't have worked Will I have worked? completed before a future time

📖 PRESENT TENSES

Present Simple

She works in an office.
The sun rises in the east.

Present Continuous

She is working right now.
I am studying for my exam.

Present Perfect

She has worked here since 2015.
I have finished my homework.

Present Perfect Continuous

She has been working for three hours.
I have been waiting for an hour.

📜 PAST TENSES

Past Simple

She worked yesterday.
I went to Paris last year.

Past Continuous

She was working when I called.
They were sleeping at 10 PM.

Past Perfect

She had worked before she left.
I had eaten when he arrived.

Past Perfect Continuous

She had been working for two hours when I arrived.

🔮 FUTURE TENSES

Future Simple (will)

She will work tomorrow.
I will call you later.

Future (going to)

She is going to work tomorrow.
I am going to study medicine.

Future Continuous

She will be working at 5 PM tomorrow.

Future Perfect

She will have worked for 10 years by 2025.

⏰ SIGNAL WORDS FOR EACH TENSE

Tense Signal Words
Present Simple always, never, usually, often, sometimes, every day/week/year
Present Continuous now, right now, at the moment, today, this week
Present Perfect already, yet, just, ever, never, since, for, recently
Past Simple yesterday, last week/month/year, in 1990, ago, then
Past Continuous while, when, as, at that time
Past Perfect already, just, never, by the time, before, after, when
Future (will) tomorrow, next week, soon, later, in the future
Future (going to) tomorrow, next week, tonight, soon

⚠️ COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

📌 Mistake 1: Confusing Present Perfect and Past Simple
❌ I have seen that movie yesterday.
✅ I saw that movie yesterday.
(Past Simple for finished time → yesterday)
📌 Mistake 2: Forgetting the -ing in continuous forms
❌ I am work right now.
✅ I am working right now.
📌 Mistake 3: Using "will" for planned actions
❌ I will meet my friend tomorrow (if it's a plan).
✅ I am going to meet my friend tomorrow.

💡 SUMMARY & KEY POINTS

📌 Key Takeaways:
- Present Simple → habits, facts, routines
- Present Continuous → actions happening now
- Present Perfect → past actions with present relevance
- Past Simple → completed past actions
- Past Continuous → actions in progress in the past
- Future (will) → predictions, promises, spontaneous decisions
- Future (going to) → plans, intentions
- Each tense has specific signal words that help identify it
- Pay attention to irregular verbs in past simple and past participle forms
