📚 INTRODUCTION

There are two ways of relating what a person has said: direct and indirect (or reported) speech.

📌 Direct Speech:
We repeat the original speaker's exact words.
He said, "I have lost my umbrella."
Remarks are placed between inverted commas, with a comma before the remark.
📌 Indirect (Reported) Speech:
We give the exact meaning without using the speaker's exact words.
He said (that) he had lost his umbrella.
No comma after "say" in indirect speech. "That" can usually be omitted after "say" and "tell + object".

📝 PRONOUN AND ADJECTIVE CHANGES

📌 A. First and second person pronouns normally change to the third person
(I → he/she, me → him/her, my → his/her, mine → his/hers, we → they, us → them, our → their)

She said, "he's my son". → She said that he was her son.
"I'm ill", she said. → She said that she was ill.
📌 B. THIS / THESE
"This" used in time expressions usually becomes "that".
She said, "She's coming this week". → She said that she was coming that week.

"This" and "that" used as adjectives usually change to "the".
He said, "I bought this pearl for my mother" → He said he had bought the pearl for his mother.

"This", "these" used as pronouns can become "it", "they/them".
He said, "I found these beside the bed" → He said he had found them beside the bed.

❓ QUESTIONS IN INDIRECT SPEECH

📌 Structure:
Direct question: He said, "Where is she going?"
Indirect question: He asked where she was going.

1. Using "ask" with indirect object:
He asked, "What have you got?" → He asked me what I had got.
He said, "Mary, when is the next train?" → He asked Mary when the next train was.

2. Question words (when, where, who, how, why, what) are repeated:
He said, "Why didn't you put on the brake?" → He asked her why she hadn't put on the brake.

📢 COMMANDS, REQUESTS, ADVICE

📌 Structure:
Direct command: He said, "Lie down, Tom".
Indirect command: He told Tom to lie down.

Verbs used: advise, ask, beg, command, order, remind, tell, warn, etc.

He said, "Get your coat, Tom!" → He told Tom to get his coat.

Negative commands: "not + infinitive"
"Don't swim out too far, boys", I said → I warned/told the boys not to swim out too far.

❗ EXCLAMATIONS IN INDIRECT SPEECH

📌 Structure:
Exclamations usually become statements. The exclamation mark disappears.
Exclamations with "What (a)..." or "How..." can be reported by "exclaim/say that":
He said, "What a dreadful idea!" → He exclaimed that it was a dreadful idea.

💡 OFFERS AND SUGGESTIONS

OFFERS

"Shall I bring you some tea?"
→ He offered to bring me some tea.

SUGGESTIONS

"Shall we meet at the theatre?"
→ He suggested meeting at the theatre.

🗣️ SAY AND TELL AS INTRODUCTORY VERBS

📌 Key Differences:
- Say can introduce a statement or follow it.
Tom said, "I've just heard the news." or "I've just heard the news", Tom said.

- Tell requires the person addressed (tell me, tell him, tell us).
He told me he was leaving.
- Exception: tell lies/stories/the truth/the time (person addressed optional).
He told (me) lies.

📋 REPORTED ORDERS (SUMMARY)

"Go to bed!"

→ He told the child to go to bed.

"Don't worry!"

→ He told her not to worry.

"Be on time!"

→ He told me to be on time.

"Don't smoke!"

→ He told us not to smoke.

💡 SUMMARY & KEY POINTS

📌 Key Takeaways:
- Direct speech uses exact words (quotation marks)
- Indirect (reported) speech reports meaning without exact words
- Tenses usually change (present → past, past → past perfect, will → would, etc.)
- Pronouns change (I → he/she, my → his/her, we → they, etc.)
- Time/place expressions change (now → then, today → that day, here → there, etc.)
- Questions use "asked + question word" (no inversion)
- Commands/requests use "told/asked + object + to + infinitive"
- Suggestions/offers use "suggested + -ing" or "offered + to + infinitive"
